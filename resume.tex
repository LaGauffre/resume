\section{Research Expertise}
\cventry{}{Numerical inversion of Laplace transform}{I work out a numerical method to recover probability density function from their Laplace transform. The desired PDF takes the form of a polynomial expansion. The method extend naturally within a multi-dimensional context and the approximation can turn into a nonparametric statistical estimation of the PDF when data are available. I widely studied the other numerical methods in the litterature and compare them to mine}{}{}{}
\cventry{}{Ruin theory}{In ruin therory, we model the financial reserves of a non life insurance company using stocastic processes. We aim to compute the probabilty that the financial reserves falls below $0$. This quantity, aka probability of ruin, is tricky to capture and motivates the use of numerical methods such as those involving Laplace transform inversion}{}{}{}
